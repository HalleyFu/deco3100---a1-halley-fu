CO2 The Silent Killer

High CO2 levels are a sign of poorly regulated air quality. This can cause headaches, fatigue, and even stress (AirGradient, 2025; Clairify, 2023; CO2Meter, n.d.). Workers can become more irritable, which can lead to poor decision-making and increased stress levels (Disruptive Technologies, 2022; uHoo, 2025). High CO2 concentrations have also been linked to reduced cognitive performance, including impaired concentration and decision-making (Castus, 2025; Tektelic, 2024). Using SAMBA data that tracks your workplace, Aerate can help you visualise the CO2 levels in your office with our Live Dashboard. In doing so, you can see how the CO2 levels are changing and you can know when to step out of the office for fresh air.

The Office Humdrum

High sound levels contribute to overstimulation and stress, leaving you tired by the end of the day. Research indicates that prolonged exposure to a noisy work environment can lead to heightened stress levels, reduced concentration, emotional fatigue, and increased irritability (Health Assured, 2025; TalktoAngel, 2025). The brain constantly attempts to filter out irrelevant auditory stimuli, an effort that can lead to cognitive overload and fatigue (TalktoAngel, 2025). SAMBDA sensors track noise activity in your office and Aerate uses this data to visualise the sound levels. In doing so, you can see how the sound levels are changing and you can know when to put on headphones and earplugs to help you relax.

Air Temperature: The first suspect

High air temperatures can lead to discomfort and fatigue, making it hard to focus. Studies have shown that when office temperatures exceed the optimal range, cognitive functions begin to decline, affecting focus, concentration, and decision-making (DBH Group, 2025; Symphony, 2025). Prolonged exposure to high temperatures also contributes to increased stress and irritability (Symphony, 2025). The physical discomfort caused by excessive heat can cause discomfort and lead to decreased productivity (Lamina Heat, 2024). SAMBA sensors track the temperature in your office and Aerate graphs this data to help you understand the temperature levels in your office with our Live Dashboard. In doing so, you can see how the temperature is changing and you can know when to adjust the thermostat or open a window for fresh air.
